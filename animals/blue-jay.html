About Blue Jay

Characteristic perky crest; blue, white, and black plumage; and noisy calls.
Weight 2.5 to 3.5 ounces.
Size 13 to 17 inches.
Speed 20-25 miles per hour.
Lifespan 15-17 years
Family crow family, Corvidae
Numbers left approximately 200,000

Fun Fact

Intelligent Birds
Intelligent:

Blue jays are known for their intelligence. They have been observed using tools, playing tricks on other birds,

Mimicry
Mimicry:

They can imitate the calls of hawks, such as the red-shouldered hawk, which helps them to deceive other birds and sometimes even humans.

Family Bonds
Family Bonds:

Blue jays are very social birds and often form tight-knit family groups. They are known to mate for life and both parents are involved in raising the young.

Molting
Molting:

Blue jays undergo a molt in late summer to early fall, during which they replace their old feathers with new ones.
