Elephant

The world's largest land animal

About Elephant

Characteristic massive bodies, large ears, and long trunks.
Weight Male Elephant(2 to 7 tons) and Female Elephant(3 to 5 tons)
Size 9 to 12ft
Speed 40 km/h
Lifespan 65 years or more
Foods (grasses, leaves, shrubs, fruits and roots) depending on the season and their habitat
Family Ursidae
Scientific name the African (Loxodonta africana) and Asian (Elephas maximus) elephants.
Numbers left approximately 415,000 African elephants and 40,000 to 50,000 Asian elephants left
species of bear African Savanna (Bush), African Forest and Asian.

Fun Fact

Communication
Communication:

They use a variety of sounds to communicate, including some that are too low for humans to hear.

Memory
Memory:

Elephants are known for their incredible memory and can remember locations of water holes over long distances and periods of time.

Trunk Versatility
Trunk Versatility:

An elephant's trunk is an incredibly versatile tool, used for breathing, smelling, touching, grasping, and producing sound. It has over 40,000 muscles!

Social Creatures
Social Creatures:

They are highly social animals and live in tight-knit family groups led by a matriarch (an older female).
