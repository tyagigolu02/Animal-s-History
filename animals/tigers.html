About Tiger

Characteristic Value
Weight 115 kg – 182 kg
Size 2.4 – 2.8 meters
Speed Up to 60 kmh
Lifespan 10 years in the wild, 17 years in captivity
Foods Deer, boar, small mammals, and elephant calves
Predators Cubs are vulnerable to Asian wild dogs and other top predators
Family Felidae
Scientific name Panthera tigris
Numbers left Between 3,000 and 4,500
Conservation Status Endangered

Fun Fact

Tigers are the largest big cat
Largest big cat

One of the largest land mammals in the world. The largest tiger ever recorded was almost 3.5 meters long and weighed nearly 453 kilograms. That’s the same weight as four refrigerators

They love to swim
Love to swim

Tiger absolutely loves to swim – and they’re good at it! In the wild and in captivity, you can expect to see the tiger spending at least a portion of their day swimming and cooling off in the river.

Tiger cubs have just a 50% chance of survival
Cubs have just a 50% chance of survival

Dependent on mothers, cubs face risks of starvation when apart, territorial male aggression, and human-caused threats when separated from their mothers.

Stripes are the fingerprints of the tiger world
Unique stripes

No two tigers have the same stripes. Each pattern is completely unique to each big cat, helping create a way to tell them apart.These strips will also appear on their skin!
